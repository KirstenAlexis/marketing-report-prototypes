Indicele Imobiliare.ro

Last updated: 15.04.2026

The Romanian real estate market opened 2026 on steadier footing. The Imobiliare.ro Index shows national apartment asking prices up 2.8% quarter-on-quarter, with București-Ilfov and Cluj setting the pace while Iași and Timișoara post modest but consistent gains. New-build supply has loosened, and mortgage demand is recovering as rates stabilise.

Search intent tells a sharper story. Queries are clustering around two-bedroom apartments under 120,000 €, with spikes for "energy-efficient" and "parking included" — affordability and running costs are beating raw square-metre maths. Investor searches for garsoniere in university cities are up 18% year-on-year, led by Cluj-Napoca, Brașov, and Oradea.

Listings move faster in Transylvania and the West; Sud-Muntenia and Sud-Est remain slower. Outside the capital, "case cu grădină" and "apartamente cu terasă" still dominate intent, a post-pandemic preference that hasn't faded. Methodology

The Imobiliare.ro Index is built from active listings on imobiliare.ro combined with notarised transaction data from ANCPI and partner banks' mortgage valuations. Prices are normalised to RON per square metre, aggregated at regional and city level using a rolling three-month median, with statistical outliers (top and bottom 2.5%) trimmed to reduce noise from mispriced or luxury listings. The Index is refreshed monthly and covers apartments, houses, land, and commercial spaces with a minimum sample of 50 listings per area — regions below that threshold are flagged as indicative only.
